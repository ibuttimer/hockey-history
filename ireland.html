Ireland Facts

Overview

On 6th February 2018, Hockey Ireland marked 125 years since its foundation in 1893. The world's first men's hockey international match was Wales v Ireland at Rhyl in January 1895.

On 27th February 2000, the Irish Ladies Hockey Union and the Irish Hockey Union merged to form the Irish Hockey Association, later Hockey Ireland.

Sources Please see Ireland Page Overview for information sources.

Organisation

There are four provincial associations affiliated to Hockey Ireland, which are responsible for hockey and its promotion and development in their respective provinces; Connacht Branch, Leinster Hockey Association, Munster Branch Irish Hockey Association and Ulster Hockey Union.

Sources Please see Ireland Page Organisation for information sources.

League Competitions

The top men's and women's teams on the island compete in their respective Irish Hockey League competitions. There are 2 Divisions; EYHL and EYHL Division2 (EYHL D2). Each year EYHL D2 is 're-constituted' based on finishing positions in the provincial leagues and the EYHL in the previous season.

Sources Please see Ireland Page League Competitions for information sources.

Cup Competitions

Hockey Ireland run numerous national cup competitions for both men and women. The Irish Senior Cup is the premier cup competition, followed by the Irish Hockey Challenge Cup, Irish Hockey Trophy and Irish Junior Cup.

In addition, teams in the Irish Hockey League compete for the EY Champions Trophy.

The Men's Senior Cup dates back to 1894 which makes it one of the oldest hockey competitions in the world.

Sources Please see Ireland Page Cup Competitions for information sources.

2018 Women's World Cup

In August 2018, the Irish women's team became the first ever Irish team to competed in a World Cup final in any team field sport. Ranked 16th in the world, Ireland were the only amateur team to compete in the 2018 World Cup. The Netherlands, the defending champions, won a record eighth World Cup crown, beating Ireland 6-0.

Sources Please see Ireland Page Ireland Page 2018 Women's World Cup for information sources.

Olympics

In 1908, the Ireland men's team played in the very first Olympic field hockey tournament. After defeating Wales 3-1 in the semi-final, they lost 8-1 to England in the final. They would have to wait 108 years before competing in their next Olympic tournament, finishing 10th in Rio de Janeiro in 2016.

The women's team played in their first Olympic's in 2020 in Tokyo, finishing 10th.
