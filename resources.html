Rules

Basic Rules
Players Maximum 11 players on the field; 10 outfield players and a goalkeepeer wearing protective equipment, or 11 outfield players. Up to 5 substitutes, and there is no limit to the number of players who are permitted to be substituted at the same time or to the number of times any player is permitted to substitute or be substituted.
Equipment Stick: Flat on one side, with a 'J'-shaped head. A player may only use the flat side to play the ball
Ball: Hard spherical with a smooth surface, between 224 mm and 235 mm, weighing between 156 grams and 163 grams, typically coloured white (or any colour which contrasts with the playing surface)
Pitch

The field of play is rectangular, 91.4 metres (100 yards) long, the side-line, and 55 metres (60 yards) wide, the backline. The goal is 3.66 metres (4 yards) wide, and located at either end, in the middle of the backline. The area enclosed by and including the two quarter circles of 14.63 metre (16 yard) radius, and the line joining them parallel to the backline in front of the goal is called the shooting circle. There are 3 lines that cut the field from side to side;

  • The centre line is located at a distance of 45.7 metres (50 yards) from the backlines.
  • Two 23-metre lines are located 22.9 meters (25 yards) from the each backline.
Scoring

A goal is awarded each time the ball is played within the circle by an attacker and does not travel outside the circle before passing completely over the goal-line and under the cross-bar.

Duration

A match consists of four quarters of 15 minutes, an interval of 2 minutes between quarter 1 and 2 and between quarter 3 and 4 and a half-time interval of 5 minutes between quarter 2 and 3.

Penalties

A free hit is awarded to the opposing team when a player commits an offence. A penalty corner is awarded to the attacking team for offences committed by the defending team within the circle. A penalty stroke is awarded to the attacking team for an offence by a defender in the circle which prevents the probable scoring of a goal.

The full rules may be found at Rules of Hockey
